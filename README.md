## Foundations & References

- [Math Stack Exchange](https://math.stackexchange.com/) – Community-driven Q&A covering all areas of mathematics.
- [arXiv Mathematics](https://arxiv.org/archive/math) – Open-access preprints in pure and applied mathematics.
- [Encyclopedia of Mathematics](https://encyclopediaofmath.org/) – Comprehensive reference for mathematical concepts and definitions.
- [OEIS](https://oeis.org/) – Online Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences.
- [PlanetMath](https://planetmath.org/) – Collaborative mathematics encyclopedia with formal definitions.

## Pure Mathematics

- [Abstract Algebra (Dummit & Foote)](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Abstract+Algebra-p-9780471433347) – Standard reference for group, ring, and field theory.
- [Topology Atlas](http://at.yorku.ca/topology/) – Extensive resource for general and algebraic topology.
- [nLab](https://ncatlab.org/nlab/show/HomePage) – Wiki-style reference for category theory and higher mathematics.
- [Stacks Project](https://stacks.math.columbia.edu/) – Open-source textbook on algebraic geometry.
- [Lean Mathematical Library](https://leanprover-community.github.io/mathlib/) – Formalized mathematics in the Lean proof assistant.

## Applied Mathematics

- [SIAM](https://www.siam.org/) – Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics resources and publications.
- [MIT OpenCourseWare – Applied Mathematics](https://ocw.mit.edu/) – Applied math lectures and notes.
- [Wolfram Demonstrations Project](https://demonstrations.wolfram.com/) – Interactive visualizations of mathematical concepts.
- [Mathematical Modeling Handbook](https://math.libretexts.org/) – Guides on applied modeling techniques.
- [Applied Mathematics Letters](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/applied-mathematics-letters) – Research journal on applied math topics.

## Linear Algebra

- [Linear Algebra Done Right](https://linear.axler.net/) – Conceptual approach to linear algebra.
- [Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors MIT OCW](https://ocw.mit.edu/) – Lecture notes and problem sets.
- [BLAS](http://www.netlib.org/blas/) – Basic Linear Algebra Subprograms for numerical computation.
- [LAPACK](https://www.netlib.org/lapack/) – Linear algebra routines for solving systems and eigenproblems.
- [NumPy Linear Algebra](https://numpy.org/doc/stable/reference/routines.linalg.html) – Linear algebra functions for Python.

## Probability & Statistics

- [Probability Theory MIT OCW](https://ocw.mit.edu/) – University-level probability courses.
- [StatLect](https://www.statlect.com/) – Clear explanations of probability and statistics concepts.
- [R Project](https://www.r-project.org/) – Statistical computing and graphics environment.
- [SciPy Stats](https://docs.scipy.org/doc/scipy/reference/stats.html) – Statistical functions for scientific computing.
- [Stan](https://mc-stan.org/) – Probabilistic programming language for Bayesian inference.

## Numerical & Computational Mathematics

- [Numerical Recipes](http://numerical.recipes/) – Classic reference for numerical algorithms.
- [Netlib](https://www.netlib.org/) – Repository of numerical software libraries.
- [SciPy](https://scipy.org/) – Scientific computing library for numerical methods.
- [Julia Math Ecosystem](https://julialang.org/) – High-performance numerical computing language and libraries.
- [PETSc](https://petsc.org/) – Scalable solvers for large-scale numerical problems.

## Optimization

- [CVXOPT](https://cvxopt.org/) – Convex optimization library for Python.
- [COIN-OR](https://www.coin-or.org/) – Open-source tools for mathematical optimization.
- [Gurobi](https://www.gurobi.com/) – High-performance commercial optimization solver.
- [PuLP](https://github.com/coin-or/pulp) – Linear programming modeling library for Python.
- [SciPy Optimize](https://docs.scipy.org/doc/scipy/reference/optimize.html) – Optimization algorithms for scientific computing.

## Symbolic Mathematics

- [SymPy](https://www.sympy.org/) – Python library for symbolic mathematics.
- [Maxima](https://maxima.sourceforge.io/) – Open-source computer algebra system.
- [SageMath](https://www.sagemath.org/) – Comprehensive open-source mathematics software system.
- [Maple](https://www.maplesoft.com/) – Symbolic and numeric computation environment.
- [Mathematica](https://www.wolfram.com/mathematica/) – Computational platform for symbolic math and visualization.

## Visualization & Plotting

- [Matplotlib](https://matplotlib.org/) – Python plotting library for mathematical visualization.
- [Plotly](https://plotly.com/) – Interactive plotting library for data and math visualization.
- [Desmos](https://www.desmos.com/) – Online graphing calculator for functions and equations.
- [GeoGebra](https://www.geogebra.org/) – Interactive geometry and algebra visualization tool.
- [Manim](https://www.manim.community/) – Mathematical animation engine popularized by 3Blue1Brown.

## Datasets & Open Science

- [Kaggle Datasets](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets) – Public datasets for statistics and modeling.
- [OpenML](https://www.openml.org/) – Open datasets and experiments for machine learning and statistics.
- [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/) – Open repository for research data and publications.
- [Figshare](https://figshare.com/) – Platform for sharing research datasets.
- [Dryad](https://datadryad.org/) – Curated scientific data repository.

## Learning Resources

### Tutorials
- [Khan Academy Mathematics](https://www.khanacademy.org/math) – Beginner-friendly math tutorials.
- [MIT OpenCourseWare – Mathematics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/mathematics/) – Free university-level math courses.
- [3Blue1Brown](https://www.3blue1brown.com/) – Visual explanations of advanced math concepts.

### Guides
- [Paul’s Online Math Notes](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/) – Clear explanations of calculus and differential equations.
- [Better Explained](https://betterexplained.com/) – Intuitive explanations of mathematical ideas.
- [MathWorld](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/) – Comprehensive mathematical reference.

### Courses
- *Calculus I–III* – Limits, derivatives, integrals, and multivariable calculus.
- *Linear Algebra* – Vector spaces, matrices, and linear transformations.
- *Probability & Statistics* – Random variables, distributions, and inference.
